What is Peer Pressure?

Peer pressure is the influence your peers can have on you. It can be both positive and negative, and recognizing it is the first step to handling it well.

Types of Peer Pressure

Spoken

Direct verbal influence.

Example: A friend says, "Just try this cigarette once."

Unspoken

Pressure felt without words.

Example: Everyone else skips class, so you feel you should too.

Positive

Encouragement to do good things.

Example: Friends cheer you on to study harder or join a club.

Negative

Influence to do harmful actions.

Example: Friends pressure you into cheating or bullying.

How to Handle Peer Pressure

  • Say “No” confidently.
  • Hang out with people who respect your choices.
  • Practice what to say ahead of time.
  • Talk to an adult you trust.
  • Walk away from situations that feel wrong.
